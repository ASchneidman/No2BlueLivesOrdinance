What is Blue Lives Matter Ordinance?

Chicago's Blue Lives Matter Ordinance, also known as Ordinance 4878, proposes that any remotely physical interaction with police officers or state officials should be considered a hate crime punishable by jail time and fines.

What is a Hate Crime?

A crime that violates the victim's civil rights and that is motivated by hostility to the victim's race, religion, creed, national origin, sexual orientation, or gender.

Facts

  • A direct violation of our First Amendment right - suppressing political dissent and hindering pathways to police accountability in Chicago.
  • Completely unnecessary addition - given already existing laws make physical interactions with an officer punishable with more than 6 months of jail and a $2500 fine.
  • Makes a mockery of Hate Crimes statuteintended for people who can’t change characteristics that are the basis for discrimination and violence.
